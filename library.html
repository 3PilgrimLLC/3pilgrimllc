The 3 Pilgrim Public Library is an evolving archive of independent research papers and technical notes. Each document reflects a self-contained investigation within mathematics, finance, psychology, or information theory. All papers are hosted permanently on Zenodo via DOI for reliable access, citation, and download. These works are freely available for review, citation, and academic discussion.

Introduces reproduction as the temporal primitive of genomic time, ego* as a locally optimizing behavioral process, and population-level bias as an emergent structural property. Non-teleological; descriptive only; no normative or predictive claims. PDF The Temporal Lattice of Fiat Stability Economics Introduces rate of sovereign debt duration compression (κ) as a structural primitive for fiat instability. Formalizes temporal lattice dynamics and stability decay via dS/dt = -S · κ, with TSR = WAM / σ_t as diagnostic. Empirical correlations from U.S. Treasury data (1950–2025) show ~70-month inflection linked to volatility spikes. Reductionist hypothesis; no predictive claims. PDF Primitive Asymmetries of Persistence 1.0 AI Research A minimal structural lattice of three primitives explaining systematic miscalibration and risk accumulation in persistent digital environments. The reductionist framework rejects psychological or adversarial explanations and attributes failure to architectural mismatch between human cognition and infrastructure enforcing temporal agnosticism, perfect persistence, and context-stripped invariance. PDF The Compute Efficiency Frontier: Why Bigger Models Hit Physical Boundaries AI Research Defines six physical and informational walls—Compute, Power, Heat, Data, Parallelism, and Transmission—that constrain AI scaling laws. Explains why Moore’s Law has collapsed and why brute-force expansion now delivers diminishing returns. Includes a visual of the walls converging on the CEF. PDF Semiotic Frustration in Machine Learning AI Research Identifies a semiotic conflation in ML discourse where “dimensionality” refers to vector/parameter count rather than true independent axes. Proposes a reductionist taxonomy with vector aggregation and true dimensional expansion primitives to resolve the paradox and unify scaling observations. PDF Toward a Structural Model of Relationship Compatibility Psychology Proposes a first-principles, systems-theoretic model of relationship compatibility based on cognitive mode selection (ERM/LRM), constraint topology, and PLR behavioral gradients. Reframing compatibility as structural alignment under shared constraints, the framework explains predictable failure modes and stability conditions for researchers, clinicians, and AI systems. PDF The Half Life of Fiat: A Statistical History of Monetary Decay Economics Analyzes the historical lifespan of fiat currencies post-decoupling from commodity backing. Across 21 cases spanning eight centuries, identifies a generational half-life (~31 years) before collapse, redenomination, or hyperinflation. PDF A Quantitative Model for Pricing Gold Economics / Metrology Reframing gold not as a speculative asset or store of value, but as a metrological invariant—a fixed reference mass—against which fiat currency drift can be measured. Provides a simple, reductionist model for calculating a theoretical intrinsic equilibrium value for gold based on three interlocking calibration ratios (GDP–gold, labor–gold hours per ounce, monetary base–gold).
